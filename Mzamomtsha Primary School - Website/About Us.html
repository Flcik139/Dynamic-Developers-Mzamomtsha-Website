Our vision at Mzamomtsha Primary School is to empower students to acquire, demonstrate, articulate and value knowledge and skills that will support them, as life-long learners, to participate in and contribute to the global world and practice the core values of the school:
Respect, Tolerance & Inclusion, and Excellence.

Our mission is to develop students with active and creative minds, a sense of understanding and compassion for others, and the courage to act on their beliefs. We stress the total development of each child: Spiritually, Morally, Intellectually, Socially, Emotionally, and Physically.

Biography of Mzamomtsha Primary School

Mzamomtsha Primary School is located between Khayelistha Township and uMfuleni Township in the Western Cape Province of the Republic of South Africa. The school's headmaster, Mr. Simphiwe Khuze, joined in 2007. The school is located in a semi-township called Drift Sands which started off as an informal settlement. Drift Sands has since been developed and with support from the South African government it turned into a sprawling township with state of the art housing units. Mzamomtsha Primary School is plays a key role in Drift Sands and is supporting the local community. The school was initially created for primary school students from Grade 1 to Grade 7, but due to the rapid population growth (births and migration) the school is also enrolling higher grades. Students are allowed up to Grade 12 - which is the Matric year. Mzamomtsha School in the remote township of Driftsands has a severe shortage of classrooms and chairs. This follows an early 2018 instruction from the district office for the school to accommodate children who could not fit into overcrowded Mfuleni schools. The Department of Education promised the school mobile classes to help handle the increased learner population, but these have still had not been delivered. Parents organised a protest to highlight the school’s needs, but nothing has changed. Frustrations are growing, and parents are even contemplating closing down the school permanently as they feel the learning conditions their children are enduring are unacceptable.

The Principal of Mzamomtsha Primary School

Simphiwe Khuze is a UWC Graduate who obtained 3 Qualifications at the University. He studied Bachelor of Arts (BA)(1990-1992), Higher Diploma in Education (1993-1993) and Bachelor of Education (199-1994). He started teaching at Mfuleni Combined School as a Teacher (Mar 1994-Apr 1998), he then moved to Manzomthombo SSS in Apr 1998 and left the school in Dec 2000. In Nov 2001, he started marking NSC History Papers, became the Chief Marker and Internal Moderator. He then went back to the school as Head of Department for 2 years. In Apr 2004, he became the Deputy Principal of Manzomthombo SSS, he resigned in Dec 2017 after being offered to be the Principal of Mzamomtsha Primary School. He started at Mzamomtsha in Jan 2018, and has been doing a good job in changing the school around.
